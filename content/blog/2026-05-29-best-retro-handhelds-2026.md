## List 1 — PS2-Capable Handhelds with Preloaded Game Options

> ⚠️ **Preloaded Games Disclaimer:** No major manufacturer legally preloads PS2 ROMs. The preloaded pathway involves buying a third-party microSD card (AliExpress, RedProClub, gogamegeek) containing unlicensed ROMs. The hardware itself is fully legal; the ROM content is not licensed by original publishers.

### Anbernic RG556 — ~$159–199 + $40–80 card

- **Screen:** 5.48" AMOLED · 1080p · 60Hz · 550 nits · 100% DCI-P3
- **Chipset:** Unisoc T820 (6nm) · Mali-G57 MC4 · 8GB LPDDR4X
- **Storage:** 128/256GB UFS 2.2 + microSD
- **Battery:** 5,500 mAh · ~4.5h PS2
- **PS2 Performance:** 1× native · NFS playable (not upscaled)
- **Preload path:** AliExpress 512GB cards with ~80K games including ~127 PS2 titles
- **Verdict:** Best "open the box and play" PS2 option under $300. Most widely documented preloaded card ecosystem.

### Anbernic RG406H — ~$157–187 + $30–80 card

- **Screen:** 4.0" IPS LCD · 960×720 · 4:3 ratio · Touch · 120Hz
- **Chipset:** Unisoc T820 (6nm) · Mali-G57 · 8GB LPDDR4X
- **Storage:** 128GB UFS 2.2 + microSD
- **Battery:** 5,000 mAh · ~4h PS2
- **PS2 Performance:** 1× native · NFS Underground playable
- **Preload path:** Amazon bundles with 128GB (4,423 games); AliExpress 512GB–1TB PS2 cards
- **Verdict:** More compact alternative to the RG556 with the same PS2 muscle. The 4:3 screen is perfect for retro content.

### Retroid Pocket 5 — ~$199 + $40–80 card

- **Screen:** 5.5" AMOLED · 1080p · 60Hz · 500 nits
- **Chipset:** Snapdragon 865 (7nm) · Adreno 650 · 8GB LPDDR5
- **Storage:** 128GB UFS 3.1 + microSD
- **Battery:** 5,000 mAh · 6–8h PS2 · 15h+ retro
- **PS2 Performance:** 2–3× upscaled · NFS smooth at 2×
- **Preload path:** 3rd-party AliExpress/TikTok reseller 512GB cards (Retroid ships no games)
- **Verdict:** Sweet spot for NFS Underground. Snapdragon 865 handles PS2 at 2× resolution where Unisoc T820 devices can't. NFS looks noticeably sharper.

### Retroid Pocket G2 — ~$199–250 + card

- **Screen:** 5.5" AMOLED · 1080p · 60Hz
- **Chipset:** Snapdragon G2 Gen 2 · Adreno 720 · 8GB LPDDR5X
- **Storage:** 128GB + microSD
- **Battery:** 5,000 mAh · 27W charging · ~8h PS2
- **PS2 Performance:** 2.5× upscaled · NFS Excellent
- **Preload path:** Same 3rd-party card ecosystem as RP5
- **Verdict:** The new mid-range king. Nearly doubles the RP5's PS2 performance. Reviews say it rivals the pricier Odin 2. Best value champion for NFS needs.

### AYN Odin 2 Portal — ~$329–499 + card

- **Screen:** 7.0" AMOLED · 1080p · 120Hz · 800 nits · 155% sRGB
- **Chipset:** Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 · Adreno 740 · 8/12/16GB LPDDR5x
- **Storage:** 128GB / 512GB / 1TB UFS 4.0 + microSD
- **Battery:** 8,000 mAh · ~10h PS2 at 3×
- **PS2 Performance:** 3–4× upscaled · NFS Flawless
- **Preload path:** 3rd-party microSD cards; AYN ships no games
- **Verdict:** The reference PS2 machine. NFS Underground at 3–4× resolution on a 7" 120Hz OLED with 10 hours of battery is as good as portable PS2 gets.

### KONKR Pocket Fit — ~$239–269 + card

- **Screen:** 6.0" LCD · 1080p
- **Chipset:** Snapdragon G3 Gen 3 (or 8 Elite +$30) · 8GB+
- **PS2 Performance:** 2–3× upscaled · NFS Excellent
- **Verdict:** Under-the-radar PS2 powerhouse. Strong chip at a competitive price. LCD instead of OLED is the main trade-off vs. Retroid/AYN options.

## Key Things to Know Before Buying

- **PS2 Emulator:** Use NetherSX2 (community fork of AetherSX2) on all Android devices listed here.
- **NFS specifics:** Underground 1/2 and Most Wanted use heavy alpha blending and motion blur. Unisoc T820 devices (RG556, RG406H) must run at 1× native. Snapdragon 865+ devices handle 2–3× upscaling.
- **Preloaded cards:** No vendor lists specific game titles to avoid IP takedowns. Ask the seller directly or check YouTube/TikTok demos before buying. Cards range from $25 (128GB) to $100+ (1–2TB).
- **Price volatility:** 2026 RAM/flash shortages have caused price hikes (RP6 went from $229→$244 in March 2026). Cross-check at time of purchase.
- **Stock Android on Anbernic:** Reviewers recommend installing Daijishō or ES-DE as a game launcher — Anbernic's stock UI is clunky. Budget ~1 hour for initial setup.
