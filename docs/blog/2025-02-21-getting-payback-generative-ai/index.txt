10:T1046,<p>Last wee I was able to attend to a webinar called "Getting Payback from Generative AI" and I wanted to share with you
the main points that were discussed. It was hosted by the MIT Sloan Management Review and presented
by <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/ramar/">Rama Ramakrishnan</a>.</p>
<p>This post is basically The notes I took during the webinar and somme recommendations as conclusion for small companies
that want to start with LLMs and Generative AI, so I hope you find them useful.</p>
<p><strong>How LLMs Work:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>LLMs work like "auto complete" systems that predict next words based on probabilities.</li>
<li>They generate text by sampling from many possible next words, so answers can vary each time.</li>
<li>They sometimes make mistakes or produce wrong information.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Three Main Methods to Adapt LLMs for Business:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><em>Prompting</em>: Ass simple instructions to the model with a clear text. This approach is good for simple, everyday tasks.</li>
<li><em>Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)</em>: Adding relevant data and context to the prompt for more accurate and current
answers.</li>
<li><em>Instruction Fine-Tuning</em>: Train the model with domain-specific examples for tasks that need high accuracy.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>The Generative AI Cost Equation:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Compare the cost of using generative AI (including adaptation and error checking) with the cost of doing the work
without AI.</li>
<li>Consider the cost of mistakes (legal, reputation, ...) and make sure your business can handle it.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Building and Evaluating Tests:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Divide complex workflows into smaller tasks.</li>
<li>Test LLM solutions on a small scale and evaluate their performance.</li>
<li>Iterate and improve, keeping a human in the loop for error checking.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Value and Limitations:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>LLMs can improve efficiency in tasks. For example writing, coding, customer support, and marketing.</li>
<li>They are not perfect; they might produce errors or miss logical steps, evaluation and error correction is
needed.</li>
<li>As technology improves, costs of adaptation and usage are decreasing, making LLMs more attractive over time.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Suggestions for Small Companies to Get Payback from Generative AI</h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Start Small:
Identify simple tasks (drafting emails, generating social media posts, or data summaries) where LLMs can be
used with simple prompting.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use Ready-Made Solutions:
Consider commercially available AI tools that already include advanced prompting and error-checking mechanisms.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Test Projects:
Run small tests for specific tasks before scaling up. This helps understand benefits and uncover issues.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Break Down Processes:
Divide business processes into smaller tasks, so you can apply AI where it is most efficient.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Evaluate Costs Carefully:</p>
<ul>
<li>Compare the cost of using AI (including adaptation and error checking) with your current process costs.</li>
<li>Consider the risk or "cost of a miss" for tasks where errors can be expensive.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Keep Human in the Process:
Always include a human in the loop, to review and correct AI outputs.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Invest in Prompt Engineering:
Spend time on designing clear instructions to get the best possible output from the AI.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Revisit and Iterate:</p>
<ul>
<li>Regularly review the performance as LLM technology improves and costs decrease.</li>
<li>Update your processes and tests based on new capabilities and insights.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Explore Open-Source Options:
Look into open-source LLMs that might be adapted to your needs with lower investment.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>If you are interested in the topic and want to dive a little more on it, I recommend you to check this
paper <a href="https://s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/marketing.mitsmr.com/events/MITSMR-Webinar-Ramakrishnan-2025-Prereading.pdf">A Practical Guide to Gaining Value From LLMs</a>
that the author share with the participants.</p>
